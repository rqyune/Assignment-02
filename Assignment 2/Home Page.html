Credit 101

  • Wait, What Even is Credit?

    Think of it like this: credit is basically your financial reputation score. It's a number (300-850) that tells lenders 'hey, this person is good at paying back money. Start building it early, and you'll thank yourself later when you want to rent an apartment or buy a car.

  • Credit Score

    Your score goes up when you pay on time and keep your balance low (under 30% of your limit is ideal). It goes down when you miss payments or max out your card. Simple as that.

  • Billing Cycle

    Here's the deal: you get a billing cycle (usually 30 days) to make purchases. Then you get a statement with everything you spent. Pay it off in full before the due date? Zero interest. That's the magic move right there.

  • Safety and Perks

    Real talk: credit cards are actually safer than debit cards. If someone steals your info, it's the bank's money at risk, not yours. Plus you get rewards, purchase protection, and you're building that all-important credit score.
